A Seat at the Tabletop

In the world of tabletop gaming, one of the most important yet most aggravating steps is finding a group of like minded individuals to gather around the table and play. Especially if you are hoping to meet regularly and evolve as a gamer. "A Seat at the Tabletop" is here to help gamers find the friends they need so they can have more time playing the games they love. The current apps available to TRPG players tend to take a clinical approach to information tracking and stat building. They are aimed at helping create characters, keeping varying story lines untangled, or allowing game masters to quickly index details for later reference. There is a multitude of apps to help players play.

What makes “A Seat at the Tabletop” standout is its focus on helping tabletop players find one another before the games even begin.

A User's Story

To get started, I developed a simple user story- As a user, I want to be able to locate a game near by and join, allowing me to keep tabs on it throughout the day. I imagined users would be accessing the app at various points throughout their week so it needed to be accessible whether that person was on their commute, in the bathroom, or lying in bed. With this, I began rough sketching some screens and doing some initial user interviews and testing. I also began to dig into the competition to see what people already were using.

Back to Basics

With a new direction and renewed vision, I created a straightforward and specific story to focus on.

As a user, I want to find a game in my area and join it, so that I can track it under my account.

Where to Next

I still feel there is so much more to add to this app. I'm extremally pleased with the final product, especially after shifting away from designing Native Apps and instead focusing on one cohesive app. However, as I mentioned in my original concept; I want “A Seat at the Tabletop” to become that one-stop-shop for gamers. For instance, I would love to see stat tracking and online meet up features eventually. For now though, I think this app should fill a real need in the current market.

It's all about that crucial first step- finding yourself a seat.
