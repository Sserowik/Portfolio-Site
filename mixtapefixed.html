MixTape

The art of the mixtape has been lost to time. With advancements in technology and the evolution of the music industry, the era of shaping our own musical narratives has passed and left behind is a pale shadow made of disorganized, unrefined playlists.

MixTape is going to help us get back to making our own soundtracks. Our own experiences.

My Process

Ideation

So how do I bring all of this to a digital space? While my experience with current music apps had been soured, it had not driven me off completely. I wanted the convenience of having all my music with me, just with the added comforts and joys of my old mix tapes. I began the process of designing an app that recreated the old charm of creating a mix tape with the modern conveniences of a mobile device.

  • “Tapes” would have a maximum length they could be. If you could find 100 songs that fit in the time frame, then more power to you, but you more than likely will find yourself having to really think about what makes the cut.
  • Once a “Tape” was made, it was un-editable. There may be some wiggle room for “copying tapes” or adding to it if there was still “room” on the tape but even those were taboo acts within the mixtape community so they wouldn't be immediate “need to have” features.
  • Sharing a tape with a friend would have limits of some kind. Maybe you only have a few copies to go around or perhaps some type of collateral would be required; akin to the idea of “trading tapes.”
  • There must be some type of customization feature. Tape color, stickers, markers, etc. Labeling tapes will be a must but also some type of “skins” feature would be a fantastic perk.

Conclusion

With MixTape, I feel I have rekindled my excitement for mixing and sharing music again. Where other apps had started to let me down, this one has made me confident we can recapture some of what has been loss in the move to digital. The evolution of music consumption has definitely lead to necessary and ingenious innovations and changes. Its all the more impressive for an industry that has some bad habits it infamously has trouble shaking. But I think we may have lost a little too much in this particular part of the community, the part where we share what we like and help provide context to others so they can hear songs the same way we do. MixTape, with its tape creation features and a unique sharing experience, can get us back there.
